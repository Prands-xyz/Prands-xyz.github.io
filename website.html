About Me

I'm Franz Justin Pendon Fegarido I'm a 2nd year student at STI Tacurong, and honestly, I'm just a guy who grew up asking too many questions and never really stopped. I was raised by two amazing people my father, Mr. Agustin L. Fegarido Jr. , and my mother, Mrs. Lina P. Fegarido who taught me the value of hard work, curiosity, and never being afraid to dream big. Everything I do today is a reflection of the values they instilled in me, and I carry that with me in everything I pursue. I got into web development not because it was the "cool" thing to do, but because I genuinely fell in love with the idea that you could build something from nothing just you, a laptop, and an idea. There's something really magical about writing a few lines of code and watching it come to life on a screen. That feeling never gets old for me. I focus on building applications that feel natural and easy to use, because I believe that the best technology is the kind that doesn't make you think too hard it just works, and it works beautifully. I'm not the kind of person who likes to keep things to himself. If I learn something new, my first instinct is to share it. I love contributing to open-source projects, not just because it looks good on a resume, but because there's something deeply fulfilling about giving back to a community that has given me so much. I've learned more from other developers, strangers on the internet, and collaborative projects than I ever could have on my own. Outside of coding, I'm pretty much just a regular person trying to enjoy life. I play guitar not because I'm some kind of musical genius, but because it's one of the few things that completely clears my head. There's something about music that speaks to a part of me that code just can't reach. I'm also really drawn to art. I love how art can say something without using a single word, and that kind of thinking actually influences how I approach design and development too always asking, "Does this feel right? Does this look human?" I spend a lot of my free time reading tech blogs and exploring new web technologies, not because I feel pressured to, but because I genuinely enjoy it. The tech world moves fast, and honestly, I love the chase. Every new framework, every new tool, every new idea it all excites me in a way that's hard to explain to people who aren't into this stuff, but if you are, you know exactly what I mean. I'm still figuring a lot of things out and that's okay. I'm 2nd year student with big dreams, a supportive family, a guitar that needs more practice, and a laptop that's seen better days. But I show up every single day ready to learn, ready to build, and ready to grow. I'm not trying to be the best in the room. I'm just trying to be better than I was yesterday and that's enough for me.
